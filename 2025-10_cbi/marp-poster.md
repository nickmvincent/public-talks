  <h1>Collective Bargaining in the Information Economy</h1>
  <h2>Can Address AI-Driven Power Concentration</h2>
  <h5><strong>Nicholas Vincent</strong><span class="super">1</span> &bull; <strong>Matthew Prewitt</strong><span class="super">2</span> &bull; <strong>Hanlin Li</strong><span class="super">3</span></h5>

</div><div></div></div>

<div class="columns-box">
  <div class="box">
    <h3 class="h3-noline">Why Now?</h3>
    <p>AI accelerates value extraction while information producers lose leverage.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="box">
    <h3 class="h3-noline">Core Idea</h3>
    <p>Intermediaries negotiate collective terms for data, adding healthy friction.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="box">
    <h3 class="h3-noline">Goal</h3>
    <p>Keep incentives to create trustworthy information and distribute power.</p>
  </div>
</div>

<section class="box">
  <h3 class="h3-noline">What Is CBI?</h3>
  <div class="columns3-np">
    <div>
      <h4>Data Creators</h4>
      <p>Researchers, writers, artists, and communities contribute the raw material.</p>
    </div>
    <div>
      <h4>Data Intermediary</h4>
      <p>Coordinates membership, tracks provenance, and negotiates enforceable terms.</p>
    </div>
    <div>
      <h4>AI Builders</h4>
      <p>Labs, model developers, and deployers commit to transparent, compensated use.</p>
    </div>
  </div>
  <ul>
    <li>Collective negotiation keeps creators from being picked off individually.</li>
    <li>Structured agreements cover compensation, limits, and transparency.</li>
    <li>Information ecosystems stay sustainable for both creators and AI.</li>
  </ul>
</section>

<div class="columns-main">
  <div>
    <h3>The Problem</h3>
    <h4>Information Markets Fail</h4>
    <ul>
      <li>Information is cheap to copy, hard to exclude, and pricing collapses toward zero.</li>
      <li>Producers struggle to capture value, encouraging secrecy, IP fights, and monopolies.</li>
      <li>Centuries of workarounds left structural incentives unresolved.</li>
    </ul>
    <h4>AI Makes It Worse</h4>
    <ul>
      <li>More capable models extract value faster from unprotected data.</li>
      <li>Traditional protections erode, accelerating uncompensated use.</li>
      <li><strong>Risk:</strong> extreme power concentration, a potential "capital singularity."</li>
    </ul>
    <h3>The Stakes</h3>
    <div class="columns2-np">
      <div>
        <h4>Political and Moral</h4>
        <ul>
          <li>Democratic instability from labor disruption.</li>
          <li>Narrowed creative and cultural diversity.</li>
          <li>Homogenized moral and social norms.</li>
          <li>Feedback loops that accelerate concentration.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div>
        <h4>Technical</h4>
        <ul>
          <li>Fragile information ecosystems.</li>
          <li>Less reliable, lower-quality models.</li>
          <li>Unpredictable harms from opaque systems.</li>
          <li>Loss of diverse, provenance-rich data sources.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </div>

  <div>
    <h3>The Solution</h3>
    <h4>CBI Creates Friction To:</h4>
    <ul>
      <li>Prevent unchecked value extraction by powerful actors.</li>
      <li>Maintain incentives to produce high-quality information.</li>
      <li>Distribute bargaining power more evenly across ecosystems.</li>
    </ul>
    <h4>Benefits</h4>
    <ul>
      <li>Higher quality data yields better, safer AI models.</li>
      <li>Protects journalism, Wikipedia, and other data commons.</li>
      <li>Improves provenance, transparency, and accountability.</li>
    </ul>
    <h3>Immediate Actions</h3>
    <div class="columns2-np">
      <div>
        <ul>
          <li><strong>Information creators:</strong> form or join intermediaries to manage data flows.</li>
          <li><strong>Technical community:</strong> ship attribution, valuation, and control tooling.</li>
          <li><strong>Regulators:</strong> create antitrust safe harbors for collective bargaining.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div>
        <ul>
          <li><strong>AI safety &amp; policy:</strong> treat CBI as core power-balancing infrastructure.</li>
          <li><strong>Tech companies:</strong> participate in good-faith CBI to improve trust and quality.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </div>

  <div>
    <h3>Technical Foundations</h3>
    <div class="columns2">
      <div>
        <h4>Data Valuation &amp; Attribution</h4>
        <ul>
          <li>Influence functions and data Shapley.</li>
          <li>Robust data attribution methods.</li>
          <li>Scaling studies for counterfactual value.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div>
        <h4>Data Control &amp; Robustness</h4>
        <ul>
          <li>Federated learning and consent infrastructure.</li>
          <li>Provenance tracking and traceability.</li>
          <li>Poisoning and adversarial research that clarifies safeguard costs.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
    <blockquote><strong>Together, these methods ground bargaining in credible counterfactual evidence.</strong></blockquote>
    <div class="box">
      <h3 class="h3-noline">CBI Is Already Emerging</h3>
      <ul>
        <li>Data licensing deals (e.g., Reddit, news publishers with OpenAI).</li>
        <li>Labor negotiations centered on AI (SAG-AFTRA, WGA, and others).</li>
        <li>Artist coalitions and opt-out movements.</li>
      </ul>
      <p><strong>Coordinated coalitions can turn scattered deals into systemic change.</strong></p>
    </div>
